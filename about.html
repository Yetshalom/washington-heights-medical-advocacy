ABOUT

Free community-based healthcare navigation in NYC

NYC Medical Advocates is a volunteer program helping people navigate the healthcare system: where to start, who to call, what to ask, and what documents to prepare. We do not provide medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment. We are not an emergency service.

100% free community-based support • Not medical advice • Not for emergencies
Request help Start here Directory

What we help with

  • Finding the right starting point for care (clinics, programs, coverage)
  • Scheduling appointments and preparing questions
  • Medical bills, denials, and paperwork navigation
  • Medication cost questions and official assistance options
  • Connecting to mental health and community resources

How it works

  1. You request help (online form, call, or text).
  2. A volunteer follows up to understand your goal and urgency.
  3. We provide practical next steps and trusted resources.

Emergency: 911 • Mental health crisis: 988

Resource standards

We prioritize official and reputable sources (NYC.gov, NY State, NYC Health + Hospitals, and recognized public-interest organizations). Programs and eligibility can change; confirm details with the provider.

Safety notice
Emergency: call 911. Mental health crisis: call or text 988. This site provides general information and navigation support only. It is not medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment.
